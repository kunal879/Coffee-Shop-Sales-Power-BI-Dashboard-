# Coffee Shop Sales Dashboard

The Coffee Shop Sales Analysis project was undertaken to gain a deeper understanding of sales performance, customer purchasing behavior, and overall business trends across multiple coffee shop locations. Using MySQL as the primary data analysis tool, the project transformed raw sales data into meaningful insights that support better business decisions. The process began with a thorough review of the dataset, followed by preparing and cleaning the raw data, creating the database, importing files, correcting data types, and validating data quality. Once the data was ready, SQL queries were developed to answer key business questions, and the results were organized for reporting and dashboard creation.

Throughout the project, a variety of MySQL functions and techniques were used to analyze the data effectively. Functions such as STR_TO_DATE(), SUM(), COUNT(), AVG(), ROUND(), MAX(), MIN(), MONTH(), DAY(), DAYOFWEEK(), and HOUR() helped extract valuable information from sales transactions. Advanced concepts including LAG() window functions, CASE statements, GROUP BY, ORDER BY, JOINS, and SUBQUERIES were utilized to identify trends, compare performance across months, and generate actionable insights.

The analysis focused on three key business areas: Sales, Orders, and Quantity Sold. Monthly sales were analyzed to measure revenue growth and identify month-over-month performance changes. Order trends were examined to understand customer demand and purchasing frequency, while quantity sold analysis provided visibility into product movement and consumption patterns. These KPIs helped evaluate overall business performance and highlighted areas requiring attention or improvement.

To make the insights easy to understand and visually appealing, several interactive dashboard visualizations were designed. A Calendar Heat Map highlighted daily sales performance, enabling quick identification of high and low-performing days. Weekday versus Weekend Analysis helped uncover customer buying patterns based on the day of the week. Store Location Analysis compared the performance of different stores and tracked month-over-month growth trends. A Daily Sales Trend Chart with an Average Line made it easy to identify exceptional sales days, while Product Category Analysis and Top 10 Products by Sales revealed the products driving the highest revenue. Additionally, a Day-and-Hour Heat Map provided insights into peak business hours, helping management understand customer traffic patterns and optimize operations.

Overall, this project demonstrates how data can be leveraged to tell a business story. By combining data cleaning, SQL-based analysis, KPI tracking, and interactive visualizations, the Coffee Shop Sales Analysis project delivers valuable insights that help stakeholders monitor performance, identify growth opportunities, improve operational efficiency, and make informed business decisions.
